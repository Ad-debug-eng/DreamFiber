What is an Internet Leased Line (ILL) and Does Your Office Really Need One?

June 18, 2025 4 min read DreamFiber Team

Understanding the Internet Leased Line (ILL)

As your business grows, you start noticing the limitations of a standard internet connection. Video calls buffer, cloud syncing takes hours, and productivity stalls. This is when IT professionals recommend an Internet Leased Line (ILL). But what exactly is an ILL, and is it worth the investment for your office?

The Definition of an ILL

An Internet Leased Line is a dedicated, fixed-bandwidth, symmetric data connection.

  • Dedicated: The cable running into your building is exclusively yours. No sharing with neighboring buildings.
  • Fixed-Bandwidth: You are guaranteed the exact speed you purchase at all times.
  • Symmetric: Upload speeds and download speeds are identical.

Does Your Office Really Need an ILL?

Not every business needs a leased line, but for many, it is non-negotiable. Here are the clear signs your office needs to upgrade:

1. You Rely Heavily on Cloud Computing

If your team uses Salesforce, Google Workspace, AWS, or Microsoft Azure, continuous connectivity is vital. An ILL ensures that your connection to the cloud is seamless, preventing lag and software timeouts.

2. Your Team Makes Constant VoIP and Video Calls

Voice over IP (VoIP) and video conferencing platforms (Zoom, Teams) require high upload speeds and low latency. Standard broadband causes jitter and dropped calls. An ILL provides the stable latency required for crystal-clear communication.

3. You Transfer Massive Files Daily

Architecture firms sending CAD files, media companies uploading 4K videos, and software teams pushing large codebases cannot wait hours for files to upload. Symmetric speeds mean uploading takes just as little time as downloading.

4. You Host Your Own Servers or Website

If you host your own email servers, VPNs, or customer-facing websites on-site, a standard broadband connection is a major risk. An ILL provides the permanent IP addresses and stable bandwidth needed to keep your servers online 24/7.
