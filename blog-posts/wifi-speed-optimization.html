Top 5 Router Placement Tips for Maximum Wi-Fi Speed at Home

June 8, 2020 4 min read DreamFiber Team

Why Your Wi-Fi Speed Drops

Wi-Fi signals are simply radio waves. Just like you lose FM radio reception when driving through a tunnel, your Wi-Fi signal degrades when it hits physical obstacles. DreamFiber recommends these top five router placement tips to maximize your speed.

1. Place the Router in the Center of Your Home

Wi-Fi routers broadcast their signal in all directions (like a 360-degree sphere).

  • If you place the router against an outside wall or next to a window, half of your signal is being broadcast into your yard or the street.
  • The Fix: Locate the most central room in your house. Placing the router here ensures an even distribution of the signal to all bedrooms, the living room, and the kitchen.

2. Elevate Your Router

Routers tend to broadcast their signals slightly downward.

  • Placing your router on the floor ensures most of your signal is absorbed by the ground or furniture.
  • The Fix: Place your router as high as possible. A high bookshelf, the top of a cabinet, or mounting it on the wall will provide a clear line of sight to your devices.

3. Avoid the Kitchen and Electronic Appliances

The kitchen is the worst place in your home for a Wi-Fi router.

  • Microwaves operate on the same 2.4 GHz frequency as many Wi-Fi routers. Turning on a microwave can completely sever your internet connection.
  • Large metal appliances (refrigerators, ovens) physically block radio waves.
  • The Fix: Keep the router at least 10 feet away from microwaves, cordless phones, and baby monitors.

4. Beware of Water and Concrete

Wi-Fi signals cannot pass through water or dense masonry easily.

  • Fish Tanks: A large aquarium will absorb Wi-Fi signals entirely.
  • Thick Walls: Concrete, brick, and stone walls severely reduce signal strength compared to standard drywall or wood.
  • The Fix: Position your router so the signal has to pass through doors and hallways rather than thick structural walls. Never hide a router behind a fish tank or indoor fountain.

5. Adjust the Antennas

If your router has external antennas, their position matters.

  • For a single-story home, point all antennas straight up.
  • For a multi-story home, point one antenna straight up (to broadcast horizontally) and one antenna horizontally (to broadcast vertically through the ceiling to the floor above).
