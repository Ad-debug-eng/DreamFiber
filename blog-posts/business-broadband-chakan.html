Leased Line vs. Business Broadband: Which Connection is Best for Your Company?

June 20, 2026 5 min read DreamFiber Team

Introduction to Business Connectivity

In today's digital age, a reliable internet connection is the backbone of any successful company. When upgrading your office internet, you will face a critical choice: should you choose a standard business broadband connection or invest in an Internet Leased Line (ILL)?

At DreamFiber, we help local businesses make this exact decision every day. Both options offer high-speed internet, but they serve completely different operational needs. Choosing the wrong one can lead to network bottlenecks, dropped VoIP calls, and lost revenue.

What is Business Broadband?

Business broadband is a shared internet connection. This means the bandwidth running to your office is shared with other businesses and homes in your local area.

  • Cost-Effective: It is generally much cheaper than a leased line.
  • Asymmetric Speeds: Download speeds are typically much faster than upload speeds.
  • Variable Performance: During peak traffic hours, your internet speed may fluctuate because you share the line with others.
  • Best For: Small offices, retail shops, and businesses that primarily use the internet for web browsing and basic email.

What is an Internet Leased Line (ILL)?

An Internet Leased Line is a dedicated, private connection delivered directly to your premises. You do not share this line with anyone else.

  • Dedicated Bandwidth: If you pay for 100 Mbps, you get exactly 100 Mbps, 24/7.
  • Symmetric Speeds: Your upload speed is exactly the same as your download speed.
  • Service Level Agreements (SLAs): Leased lines come with guaranteed uptime and rapid fault-repair times.
  • Best For: Mid-to-large enterprises, call centers, IT firms, and businesses relying heavily on cloud applications or heavy data transfers.

Key Differences: The Head-to-Head Comparison

To help you decide, let's break down the core differences:

  • Speed Consistency: Broadband fluctuates based on local traffic. A leased line remains constant regardless of the time of day.
  • Upload Capabilities: Broadband struggles with heavy uploads (like large video files or cloud backups). Leased lines handle heavy uploads effortlessly due to symmetric speeds.
  • Reliability and Fix Times: If your business broadband goes down, it might take days to fix. Leased lines from DreamFiber come with strict SLAs, meaning issues are resolved within hours.
  • Security: A dedicated leased line offers a highly secure, private pathway for your data, reducing interception risks compared to a shared broadband network.

Which is Right for Your Company?

Ask yourself these simple questions:

  1. Does your business lose money for every hour the internet is down?
  2. Do you host servers, use VoIP systems, or constantly upload large files to the cloud?
  3. Do you have more than 20 employees using the network simultaneously?

If you answered "Yes" to these, an Internet Leased Line is your best investment. If your business operates fine with occasional slowdowns and mostly requires basic browsing, Business Broadband will suffice.
