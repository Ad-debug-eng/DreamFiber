The True Cost of Downtime: Why Reliable Business Broadband Matters

June 14, 2023 5 min read DreamFiber Team

The Hidden Threat to Your Bottom Line

Imagine it is 2:00 PM on a Tuesday. Your office is buzzing. Suddenly, the internet drops. The CRM won't load, the VoIP phones go dead, and employees start staring at their screens.

Many business owners view internet connectivity as a basic utility, just like electricity. But treating it as an afterthought can be financially devastating. At DreamFiber, we emphasize that investing in reliable business broadband isn't an expense Ã¢â‚¬â€ it's risk management.

Calculating the True Cost of Internet Downtime

Downtime costs much more than just the hourly wage of employees who can't work. When the network crashes, the financial impact ripples across your organization:

  • Lost Sales and Revenue: If your Point of Sale (POS) system goes offline, or your sales team cannot access the CRM to close deals, you instantly lose money.
  • Plummeting Productivity: Calculate your total hourly payroll. Every hour the internet is down is an hour of wages paid for zero output.
  • Damage to Reputation: If clients cannot reach you, or if you miss critical deadlines due to an outage, they will lose trust in your business.
  • Data Loss and Recovery Time: Sudden drops in connectivity can corrupt files during transfer, leading to hours spent recovering or recreating lost data.

Why Standard Connections Fail

Why do standard broadband connections experience so much downtime?

  • Shared Infrastructure: You share cables with the surrounding area. An issue down the street can take out your office.
  • Lack of Priority Support: Standard plans do not guarantee quick fix times.
  • No Redundancy: Most standard setups only have one single point of failure. If the router or main line breaks, you are completely offline.

How Reliable Business Broadband Protects You

Partnering with a premium local ISP changes the equation entirely. Here is how reliable business broadband keeps your business running:

Strict Service Level Agreements (SLAs)

An SLA is a contract between you and your ISP guaranteeing uptime (often 99.9% or higher). If the network goes down, the ISP guarantees a fix within a set timeframe, usually 4 to 6 hours.

Built-In Redundancy (Backup Internet)

The best way to prevent downtime is to expect it. DreamFiber can configure an automatic failover system. If your primary fiber optic line gets cut by construction work, your router automatically switches to a backup connection (like a secondary broadband line or a 5G/4G cellular backup). Your team won't even notice the switch.

Localized, Rapid Tech Support

When your internet goes down, you don't want to be talking to a call center on the other side of the country. Using a local ISP like DreamFiber means our engineers are nearby and can be on-site rapidly to resolve physical hardware issues.

Conclusion: Protect Your Business Today

Downtime is expensive; reliable internet is not. Don't wait for a major outage to realize the importance of your network infrastructure. Contact DreamFiber today to upgrade to enterprise-grade, reliable business broadband with guaranteed uptime and advanced redundancy solutions.
